Why the between-season moments get their own mockup

  • These are the screens players screenshot. A manager game is loved at its peaks (going up, lifting the trophy) and respected at its troughs (the sack). Today's CareerReview/RunOverModal/JobMarket are functional but flat — same card grammar as a settings panel. Each beat now has one emotional headline moment, then business below.
  • Promotion review: decisions get budgets pinned, not footnoted. Contract renewals show a live running total bar ("£9M committed · £39M left") — today the aggregate hides in the footer and you discover the damage after committing. Academy potential keeps the fuzzy ★ range, but scouting shows the cost up front and the section explains what potential does in one line.
  • The sack: vacancies state the board's expectation. "Squad stature: modest" tells you nothing about what survival looks like. Each card now carries "Board expects: top half" — making the job choice a real risk/reward decision. Alternative considered: a browsable list of every club in the world (full FM fantasy). Rejected — at the lowest moment of a run, three readable offers beat thirty rows of choice paralysis; reputation gating already does the curation.
  • Glory: the season summary is a story, not a stat dump. Champions screen leads with the trophy and the honours strip, then three stats that summarise the season, then share. The share button reuses the existing formatRunResult — with a preview of what gets copied, fixing the blind-copy smell.
  • Confetti is suggested statically here; in product it's the existing deterministic particle burst, reduced-motion safe.
