Why this screen exists (it replaces the “Season” tab as the default landing)

  • The home screen is the next match, not a tab row. Today the player lands on Tactics/Transfers and the kick-off lives at the bottom of the Season tab, patched over with a sticky JourneyBar. This screen makes the fixture the centre of gravity: opponent, your readiness, the stakes and the kick-off button are one glance, one tap. The JourneyBar logic survives — the hero CTA swaps to “Sign players” / “Pick your XI” when the squad isn’t legal — but it's the hero of a real screen instead of a bar bolted above every screen.
  • Relative strength, not raw numbers. “ATK 643 · DEF 531” means nothing without your own numbers next to it. The edge bar compares the two sides and says the conclusion in words (“Slight favourites”). Alternative considered: FM-style betting odds (4/6, 11/2). Rejected — odds are opaque to non-gamblers and imply a precision the xG sim doesn't have; a two-colour bar + plain-English verdict reads instantly.
  • Stakes are three numbers; the arithmetic is an expander. Win/Draw/Loss payouts stay headline; interest, wages, upkeep and streak move into “How is this calculated?”. Today that arithmetic is a 10px footnote that everyone squints at or ignores. Alternative: keep the full breakdown always visible. Rejected — it's needed the first three times, then never again; an expander serves both audiences.
  • The wager is a stepper, not a 56px-wide text input. Quick chips (None / ¼ / Max) stay for the common cases.
  • Table + feed live with the fixture. The league table snippet shows your zone (±3 rows), with the full table one tap away. The inbox stops being a whole tab: messages become a feed on the home screen, with inline Accept/Reject. The current Inbox tab appears/disappears by mode, which destabilises the nav — a feed slot is mode-agnostic and empty states cost nothing.
  • Nav drops from 7 tabs to 4 (Home / Squad / Market / Club). Challenges, Compete, Records move under Club (see mockup 05 for the IA map). The tab set no longer mutates per mode — Draft simply shows “Market” as locked with a reason, instead of five tabs vanishing.
  • “New Game” leaves the header. A destructive action shouldn't be a first-class button on every screen; it lives behind the ☰ menu (which also hosts Daily, tutorial, sound). The logo stays the way back to the Start Menu.
