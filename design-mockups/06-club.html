Why the Club tab can absorb three tabs without becoming a junk drawer

  • A pill sub-nav, not nested tabs. Club / Records / Challenges / Compete / Settings are pills inside the tab — one tap deeper than today, but they stop competing with the core loop for nav space. The pills are mode-aware (a Classic run shows Records/Challenges/Compete/Settings; Career adds the hub) without the top-level nav ever changing shape. Alternative considered: a "More" overflow menu (the pre-June design). Rejected — it hid everything equally; pills keep each section one visible tap away and show a badge per section.
  • The career hub leads with identity and trajectory, not settings: crest + reputation, the pyramid with your rung lit, honours as a cabinet, then facilities and history. Settings drops to the last pill — renaming your club is a launch-day action, not a weekly one.
  • Facilities show the NEXT level's payoff ("Lvl 2 → 3: +£1.2M gate") instead of only the current effect — the upgrade decision is currently made blind.
  • Season history gets outcome chips (▲ promoted, ▼ relegated, 🏆 cup) per row, readable as a career at a glance — and it's where the in-progress domestic-cup feature (spotted in your working tree) would surface.
