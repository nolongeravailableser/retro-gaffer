How the shipped career cup fits the redesign

  • A cup tie is a Home-hero VARIANT, not a new tab. The shipped feature plays QF/SF/Final as extra midweek ties at matchweeks 6/12/18. In this IA the Home hero simply swaps grammar for those weeks: amber trim, a round tag (QUARTER-FINAL), knockout stakes instead of payouts, bracket snippet instead of the league table. Three ties a season can't justify permanent chrome. Alternative considered: a fifth "Cup" tab while the cup is alive. Rejected — it reintroduces exactly the mode-mutating nav this redesign removes, for a screen you need three times a season.
  • "Glory, not gold" must be said out loud. The engine pays no prize money for cup ties — players trained by 21 league matchweeks of payouts WILL expect one. The stakes strip says what winning gets you (the semi, reputation, the trophy → honours) and the one-line note says what it doesn't. Same point-of-use-explainer rule as everywhere else (system rule 5).
  • The real cost of a cup tie is squad condition — so surface the rotation decision. Injuries and bans carry into the league (shipped behaviour), and fatigue already exists in Career. The rotate hint ("3 starters are TIRED · league resumes at MW7") turns the feature's hidden trade-off into the interesting weekly decision it was designed to be. Alternative: auto-suggest a rotated XI silently. Rejected — making the choice visible IS the feature; one tap to "Rotate XI" keeps it effortless.
  • Full-time gets the cup variant of the receipt: no money table — progression headline, what it cost (carried injuries, with the league consequence spelled out), then the report. The "fix your XI" route matters more here than after a league game, because the next league fixture is days away with a possibly-wounded squad.
  • Already covered elsewhere: the cup title chip in season history and the Cups honours tile shipped into CareerHub — mockup 06 anticipated both. Reputation gains land in 06's meter; a Final win adds a 07-style glory moment using the existing Champions template with the cup headline.
