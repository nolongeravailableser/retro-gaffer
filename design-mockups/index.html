RETRO GAFFER — design review & redesign proposal

Standalone mockups. Nothing here touches the game code — open each page, resize the window to compare mobile (<900px) and desktop layouts, and hit “ⓘ Design rationale” on any page for the reasoning and the alternatives that were rejected.

⚠ These are throwaway visuals for evaluation. Player data is illustrative. The full written review lives in the chat alongside this folder; each page carries its own screen-level rationale.
01

Home / Match day

The biggest structural change: a fixture-first home screen replacing the Season tab + JourneyBar combo. Relative-strength edge bar, stakes with an arithmetic expander, wager stepper, table snippet, and the Inbox reborn as a feed. Nav drops 7 → 4 stable tabs; “New Game” leaves the header.

 02

Squad & tactics

Pitch-first on every viewport. Roster rows rebuilt around a single OVR badge (form ★, chem %, lettered status chips); ATK/DEF and sub-stats move into a tap-through player sheet, where Sell also now lives. Team strength + chemistry as live toolbar chips.

 03

Transfer market

The three market tiers (Free / Open / At rivals) promoted from footnote to primary segments, each teaching its own rule. Bank, wage-budget bar and squad count pinned on top. Fee buttons that say what they do. A browsable, explained window-closed state.

 04

Match flow — live + full-time

Two states side by side. Decisions (team talk, subs) become true overlays with effects shown per option — they can never sit below the fold again. Full-time becomes a matchday receipt: itemised payout, team news with a “fix your XI” route, then the report.

 05

Design system & IA map

Colour roles (green = action, amber = money, full stop), the eight rules that fix the cross-screen inconsistencies, the type floor (12px data minimum), core atoms, and the 7-tabs → 4-tabs IA map showing where everything goes.

Round 2 — full coverage (the screens round 1 skipped)

06

Club tab — the consolidation proof

Career hub (identity + reputation, lit pyramid, honours cabinet, facilities with next-level payoffs, history with outcome chips) plus the pill sub-nav that absorbs Records / Challenges / Compete / Settings without a junk drawer.

 07

Season moments — promotion · sacking · glory

The three emotional beats, each as a real moment: promotion review with pinned renewal budget and explained youth potential; the sack with board expectations per vacancy; Champions of England with a story-first summary and a share preview.

 08

Front door — start menu + onboarding

The start menu gets floodlight atmosphere and a Resume card with trajectory; the Daily gets a live hook. Onboarding shrinks to 3 steps (identity → difficulty → play), with the mechanics lecture replaced by contextual coach-marks at first contact.

 09

Draft night (Classic)

“On the clock” framing, a rival-picks ticker, a budget bar that makes the hidden XI-reserve guard visible (disabled picks say why), needs-based role chips, and a your-squad rail. Same engine, finally visible.

 10

Cup week (career) — covers the shipped domestic cup

The Home hero as a cup-tie variant (amber trim, knockout stakes, “glory not gold” stated at point of use), a bracket snippet replacing the table, a rotation hint surfacing the carried-injury trade-off, and a money-free full-time screen. No fifth tab needed.

What “modern feel” means here (without losing the retro soul)

Spec
Surfaces & depth: three elevation steps with soft gradients and shadows instead of one near-black + borders everywhere (already applied across these pages). · Motion as feedback: springy bottom-sheets for the player card, count-ups on money changes, score pops, press-scale on buttons (90–150ms), skeleton rows while lists build — and nothing looping forever; all behind prefers-reduced-motion. · Touch-first interactions: bottom sheets instead of hover tooltips, 44px minimum targets, swipe between Club pills. · States for everything: empty, loading, offline and locked states all say why and what to do next — absence is always explained. · Focus & a11y: visible focus rings, icon+letter status chips, 12px data floor, AA contrast on muted text.

The findings these address (abridged — full review in chat)

High
No relative strength anywhere. Opponents are raw “ATK 643 · DEF 531”; your own numbers are on another tab. The core pre-match question — “am I favoured?” — is unanswerable.
→ 01: edge bar + plain-English verdict.
High
Header costs ~40% of a phone screen. Logo row + club row + HUD row + buttons row before any content; the pitch lands two screens down.
→ 01/02: one-row top bar, New Game demoted to ☰.
High
Squad list can't do its job at 288px. Names truncate, ATK/DEF double-encoded ×16 rows, no OVR (the market shows OVR — the worst data inconsistency in the game), and 11 permanent Sell buttons.
→ 02: OVR rows + player sheet.
High
Match decisions can render below the fold in the scrollable feed — the game looks frozen at exactly the moment it wants input.
→ 04: modal decision overlays.
Med
7 tabs that mutate by mode. Inbox appears/disappears, Draft hides five tabs, Compete is nearly empty offline. The nav never feels stable, and 7 items force 9px mobile labels.
→ 01/05: 4 stable tabs + locked states.
Med
The economy explains itself in 9–10px footnotes. Stakes arithmetic, wage-over-budget, promotion rules — all decision-critical, all micro-type.
→ 01/03: 12px floor, expanders, wage bar.
Med
Colour does four jobs at once. Neon green = brand + active + positive + chemistry; amber = money + warnings + rarity. Tier/role/price colours have no key, and several signals are colour-only (attention dot, zone borders).
→ 05: colour roles + “never colour alone”.
Med
Hover-only help on a touch product. MiniStats meanings, scout briefs, disabled-button reasons all live in tooltips that don't exist on mobile.
→ 03/05: inline explainers at point of use.
Low
Post-match payout is a prose sentence at the end of a scroll; team news is buried under it.
→ 04: receipt + team news with a fix-XI route.
Low
Permanent logo flicker + ubiquitous glows tax attention all session and dilute the one CTA that should glow.
→ 05: motion-as-feedback rule.

What was deliberately kept

Keep
The CRT identity (palette, scanlines, mono numerals, ticker voice) · the Start Menu + Resume front door · the difficulty picker cards · the Sign→Pick→Kick-off journey logic (it becomes the Home hero's state machine) · Auto-pick/Auto-sign · the live match's interactive decisions · deterministic seeds, save codes, PvP codes · promotion confetti.
