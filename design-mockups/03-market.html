What changed vs the current TransferMarket

  • The three market tiers become the primary structure. Free agents / Open market / At rivals are the most important mental model in the market (free = safety net, open = normal buys, poach = premium + weakens a rival) — today they're a small filter row and a footnote (“free agents (under 64 OVR) are free; poaching a rival weakens them”). Each segment now carries its own one-line explainer, so the rule is taught where it applies. Alternative considered: one merged list with coloured price buttons only (status quo, lighter). Rejected — three price colours with no key is exactly the kind of implicit convention this game over-relies on.
  • Money context is pinned at the top. Bank, wage bill vs budget (as a bar — the single most missable number in the current UI, where going over budget is a 10px rose footnote on another tab), and squad count. You can't make a good buying decision without these three; they were previously spread across Season, the HUD, and a footer.
  • Fee buttons say what they do: “Sign · Free”, “Buy · £28M”, “Poach · £39M ↪ Oakvale”. The poach premium and the victim club are on the button, not in fine print.
  • Window-closed is a state, not a dead end. The list stays browsable but desaturated, with one amber banner explaining when it reopens — replacing today's silently disabled buttons with hover-only tooltips (which don't exist on touch).
  • Row economy: role · name · club/era · chem gain · OVR · fee. The 6-stat MiniStats grid comes off the card — it moves into the same player detail sheet as mockup 02 (tap a row). Comparing 60 players across six sub-stats at 9px isn't a decision aid, it's noise. Alternative: keep MiniStats but only on desktop. Rejected — divergent mobile/desktop information makes the game feel different per device, and OVR + role + chem is what the buy decision actually uses.
  • This page covers Career/League. The Classic draft shop and the Draft Room would adopt the same row anatomy and segment pattern (pack pills become segments), but are out of scope for this pass.
