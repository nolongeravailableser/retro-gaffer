What changed vs the current Tactics tab

  • One headline number per player: OVR. Today the squad list shows two bars + two numbers (ATK/DEF) per row, twice over, in a 288px column where names truncate to “San…”. The redesigned row is: role · name · status · form · chem · OVR. The full ATK/DEF/8-stat detail lives one tap away in the player sheet. The market already shows OVR; the squad list inexplicably doesn't — this fixes the worst data inconsistency in the game. Alternative considered: keep ATK/DEF columns and drop history instead. Rejected — picking an XI is a ranking task, and ranking needs one comparable number; two semi-correlated numbers force mental math 16 times per screen.
  • Sell is no longer a button on every row. Eleven permanent “Sell £2M” buttons invite accidents and eat a third of each row. Sell moves into the player detail sheet behind its existing two-tap confirm. Alternative: swipe-to-sell on mobile. Rejected — undiscoverable, and conflicts with drag-to-pitch gestures.
  • Pitch first, on every viewport. The current mobile order (squad list above pitch) was chosen so new signings are visible, but it buries the actual decision surface two screens down. The pitch is now compact enough to lead, the bench is a horizontal strip under it, and a new signing is handled by auto-assign + a toast — not by reordering the whole screen.
  • Team strength is a toolbar chip, not a panel below the fold. ATK/DEF/chemistry links sit beside the formation picker, updating as you swap players — feedback at the moment of the decision. Chemistry % shows on the slot card itself (the green +20% pip).
  • Formation picker shows the shape (segments are fine, but the active one renders its row counts — 4-4-2 → “1·4·4·2” under the pitch header). Out-of-position slots keep the amber treatment plus an explicit “−10%” in the player sheet, instead of a bare “!”.
  • Status is icon + letter, never colour alone. BAN / INJ·2 / TIRED are small lettered chips readable by colourblind players; the same chips appear identically in the roster, on slot cards, and in the match team-news panel.
  • Training folds in as a third segment (XI · Bench · Training) instead of a separate card pushed below the chemistry panel.
