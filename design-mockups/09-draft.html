What changed vs the current DraftRoom

  • Draft night gets a sense of occasion. Today the draft is a competent filter-list in a modal — functionally fine, emotionally flat. The amber "ON THE CLOCK · PICK 4/16" framing plus a rival-picks ticker ("Oakvale took Shearer £38M") makes the 11 AI clubs feel present. The engine already decides their picks — they're just never shown; this surfaces information that exists. Alternative considered: a full pick-by-pick draft board (NFL style, every club's picks in a grid). Rejected — 12 clubs × 16 rounds is a wall; a one-line ticker + their last pick gives the "living room" feel without the spreadsheet.
  • The budget bar makes the reserve guard visible. The engine quietly reserves money so you can always complete a legal XI ("pickable" logic) — players currently discover it as mysteriously disabled buttons. The bar shows spent / available / reserved for remaining XI as segments, and disabled picks say why in 9px under the price. Honest constraints beat silent ones.
  • Role coverage chips upgrade from numbers to needs. "DEF 2/4 todo" in warn-amber vs "GK 1/1 ✓" — the same data as today's header grid, but stated as what's left to do.
  • Your squad rail keeps the picks in sight (desktop right rail, mobile collapsible) with fee paid per player — today your drafted squad lives only in the header counter until the draft ends.
  • Timed picks were considered and rejected — this is a cosy nostalgia game; a shot-clock punishes the player browsing 30 years of football, which is the whole point.
