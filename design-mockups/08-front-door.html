What changed vs the current StartMenu + OnboardingModal

  • The front door gets atmosphere. At desktop the current menu is a small left-ish column floating in dead black — it reads as a settings dialog, not a game. Floodlight gradients, a glowing wordmark and a hero Resume card make the same content feel like match night. Zero new assets — it's all CSS light. Alternative considered: a stadium illustration/pixel-art backdrop. Rejected for scope — art assets need sourcing and maintenance; gradient atmosphere gets 80% of the warmth free, and stays on-theme.
  • Resume shows trajectory, not just facts. A season progress bar under "Championship · S4 · MW3" — the card answers "where was I?" before you tap.
  • The Daily gets a live hook ("today's rule · resets in 6h") on the front door — it's the retention feature, and today it's a small button in the header that most players never read.
  • Onboarding: three steps, show-don't-tell. Step 1 merges club name + kit (one identity moment, live shirt preview). Step 2 picks difficulty (the existing, excellent cards). Step 3 is NOT a 5-card lecture — it's straight into the game with three contextual coach-marks that appear on first contact (first market visit, first XI gap, first kick-off). The carousel taught chemistry/bosses/economy to people who hadn't seen a single screen yet; teaching at first contact actually lands. Alternative: keep the carousel but cut to 3 cards. Rejected — any pre-game lecture is forgotten by kick-off; the mechanics are simple enough to teach in place.
